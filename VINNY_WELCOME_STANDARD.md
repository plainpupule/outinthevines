# Vinny Welcome Standard™ — Draft Methodology v0.1

## Purpose

The Vinny Welcome Standard is an editorial framework for evaluating whether a winery demonstrates consistent, credible, and inclusive hospitality, with particular attention to LGBTQ+ travelers.

It is designed to celebrate strong hospitality, identify areas for improvement, and help travelers make informed choices without reducing a complex experience to unexplained stars.

## Core principles

1. Evidence over assumption.
2. Firsthand experience matters, but is not the only evidence.
3. Political activity is contextual and never decisive by itself.
4. A missing review is not a negative review.
5. Scores require confidence labels and review dates.
6. Wineries have a right to submit factual corrections and evidence of improvement.
7. Sponsorship never affects the designation.

## Weighted dimensions

### 1. Firsthand LGBTQ+ guest experience — 30%
Measures comfort, respect, consistency, identity-related interactions, and the overall feeling of belonging during documented visits.

### 2. Hospitality and staff conduct — 20%
Measures warmth, professionalism, equal treatment, service recovery, and consistency across staff and guest types.

### 3. Conflict-resolution readiness — 15%
Measures escalation pathways, manager response, complaint handling, harassment prevention, and relevant staff training.

### 4. Inclusive practices and representation — 15%
Measures policies, language, marketing, gender-neutral options where relevant, facilities, representation, and visible welcome signals.

### 5. Community engagement — 10%
Measures meaningful, sustained participation in LGBTQ+ or broader inclusion work, distinguishing long-term action from one-time promotion.

### 6. Public leadership actions — 10%
Measures verified endorsements, donations, statements, ownership actions, or public positions that materially relate to inclusion or exclusion. This category can never determine the designation alone.

## Evidence classes

- Firsthand visit report
- Verified guest report
- Winery interview or questionnaire
- Training record or policy document
- Official winery communication
- Public record
- Credible news reporting
- Documented incident and response
- Community-partner confirmation

## Evidence quality

Each item should be tagged for:
- Date
- Source
- Reviewer
- Independence
- Specificity
- Corroboration
- Relevance
- Recency
- Resolution status

## Confidence labels

### Low confidence
Limited or dated evidence, typically one source class or incomplete firsthand review.

### Medium confidence
Multiple sources with reasonable consistency, including at least one current firsthand or operational source.

### High confidence
Recent, multi-source evidence with strong consistency, documented winery input, and editorial review.

## Proposed designation bands

- Vinny Verified
- Highly Welcoming
- Welcoming
- Developing
- Mixed Experience
- Use Your Discretion
- Review in Progress

Final numeric thresholds should not be published until the rubric is pilot-tested across at least 10 to 15 wineries.

## Review workflow

1. Open a winery review file.
2. Assign lead and secondary reviewers.
3. Conduct at least one firsthand visit where feasible.
4. Collect public and winery-provided evidence.
5. Score each dimension using written anchors.
6. Apply evidence-quality adjustments.
7. Assign confidence.
8. Conduct secondary editorial review.
9. Send factual summary to winery for correction window.
10. Publish with review date and next scheduled review.
11. Log corrections and change history.

## Correction and appeal process

A winery may submit:
- Factual corrections
- Ownership or leadership changes
- Updated policies
- Training documentation
- Incident-response documentation
- Community partnerships
- A request for re-review

Corrections to factual errors should be handled promptly. Changes to editorial judgment require new or materially different evidence.

## Recency

Operational and training evidence should generally be refreshed annually. Public incidents may remain relevant longer when unresolved, repeated, or indicative of ongoing leadership or policy.

## Conflicts of interest

Reviewers must disclose:
- Free tastings or hosted visits
- Wine-club membership
- Personal relationships
- Paid partnerships
- Business relationships
- Prior disputes

A paid relationship does not automatically prevent coverage, but it must be disclosed and cannot influence the designation.

## Pilot recommendation

Before public scoring:
- Test the rubric on 10 to 15 Temecula wineries
- Use at least two reviewers on a subset
- Compare scoring consistency
- Refine definitions and thresholds
- Consult an attorney regarding defamation, disclosures, and appeals
- Invite feedback from LGBTQ+ travelers and hospitality professionals
